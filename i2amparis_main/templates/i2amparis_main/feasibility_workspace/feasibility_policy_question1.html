What is the trajectory of global fossil CO2 emissions based on pre-Glasgow current policies, NDCs, and pledges announced in Glasgow?

Based on modelling the policies legislated before the COP26 in Glasgow, CO2 emissions stabilise or start declining in the current decade reaching 33-38 Gt by 2030. If this trend is followed beyond 2030, emissions are further reduced to 24-30 Gt by 2050. Emission reductions are much stronger if countries follow the targets of their NDCs, reaching 30-33 Gt by 2030 and 19-23 Gt by 2050. Alternatively, if countries follow their Glasgow pledges post-2030 after reaching their NDC goals, the global emissions for 2050 will be further reduced to a range of 10-13 Gt.
